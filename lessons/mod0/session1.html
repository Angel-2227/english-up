Grammar — Verb To Be ⭐
⏱ 15 min
What is "Verb To Be"?
To be is the most important verb in English. We use it to say who we are, what we are like, and where we are. It changes depending on the subject (I, you, he…) and the time (present or past).

In Spanish: ser / estar → In English, both become to be.
🎵 Verb To Be — in the song
There he was, this young boy… → was = verb to be (past) A stranger to my eyes. → He was a stranger. I felt all flushed… → I was embarrassed. As if I wasn't there. → wasn't = was + not (negative)
📌 Full conjugation — present & past
Subject🟢 Present (now)🔵 Past (before)
I am  I am happy. was  I was happy.
He / She / It is  She is kind. was  She was kind.
You / We / They are  They are here. were  They were here.
📌 Positive · Negative · Question forms
Subject✅ Positive❌ Negative❓ Question
I (present) I am embarrassed. I am not sure.
= I'm not…		 Am I right?
He/She (present) He is a stranger. He is not here.
= He isn't…		 Is he a stranger?
They (present) They are singing. They are not here.
= They aren't…		 Are they singing?
I/He/She (past) He was a stranger. He was not there.
= He wasn't…		 Was he a stranger?
They (past) They were happy. They were not there.
= They weren't…		 Were they happy?
💡 Key rules to remember:
1. For questions, move Verb To Be before the subject: "She is kind." → "Is she kind?"
2. For negatives, add not after: "They are here." → "They are not here." (= aren't)
3. Contractions: am not → I'm not  · isn't · aren't · wasn't · weren't

    Have you ever felt embarrassed in public? What happened? Where were you?
